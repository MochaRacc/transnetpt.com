The Croydon branch becomes apart of the Archerton Line from the 12th of December

 

Overview

On December 12th, the Croydon branch (Glendurrie, Elphinstone, Scofield, Fairview Valley & Croydon) becomes apart of the Archerton Line, following community feedback and suburban service trials starting earlier this year.

Please note: The lists below detail how customers may be affected by these changes going forward.

 

What will happen to the existing line to Archerton?

The existing line (Withernsea & Stockton) will be closed permanently to cater to the new alignment/deviation. In favour, Withernsea passengers will have coach connections to Elphinstone and Stockton passengers will have a dedicated bus service to/from Croydon.

 

What are the current plans for the day prior?

- From 12:30pm, the last Regional Express service will use the Stockton - Withernsea alignment, as dedicated diesel shuttles will run operated by the Broadshore Spa Heritage Railway group.

- At 1pm, all suburban services will conclude with a dedicated steam/electric farewell service operated by the Thornsbury Rail Preservatioon Club (T.R.P.C)

- At 5pm, the steam/electric shuttle will finish as coaches replace trains for the remainder of the evening from Northam to Archerton
